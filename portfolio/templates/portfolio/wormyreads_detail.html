Project Purpose and Goal

This project included three main phases and iterations of the site. The Phase 1 involved setting up foundational elements such as user authentication, login, logout, and account management functionalities to establish a secure and personalized user experience. In Phase 2, the focus shifted towards expanding the platform by integrating a comprehensive book catalogue, allowing users to explore and discover a wide range of books conveniently. Lastly, Phase 3 centered on the development and implementation of a sophisticated wishlist feature tailored to individual user accounts, providing a unique and personalized browsing and saving experience.

I found that the most effective approach to implementing these three phases was to utilize a systematic and iterative process, leveraging feature flags to dynamically control and render the evolving functionalities of the platform based on the current development stage.

Web Stack and Explanation

The Wormyreads project progressed through three distinct phases, each pivotal in enhancing the platform's functionality. Problems and Thought Process

Like most projects, I ran into a few bumps along the way. One particularly difficult area was the organization and structure of the code because of the project's size. I realized how important maintaining an organized structure would be.

I worked hard to keep components as reusable as possible and utilized props for many slight variations. I also used styled-components because the structure of the CSS-in-JS is much clearer and prevents overrides.
